# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Wedding guests, family, and wedding-party members visiting before and during Ally Hall and Dalton Vaughan's wedding weekend in Mobile, Alabama. They are usually looking from a phone or laptop for practical details: schedule, venues, lodging, travel, registry, FAQs, and RSVP status. During the weekend, they may need fast access to addresses, times, attire, and travel context.

## Product Purpose

This site is a polished digital wedding invitation and guest guide for Ally and Dalton's April 17, 2027 wedding weekend. It should welcome guests, carry the emotional tone of the celebration, and make logistics easy to understand without feeling utilitarian. Success means guests feel cared for, prepared, and excited while the site still feels personal, formal enough for the occasion, and easy to use.

## Brand Personality

Southern, romantic, editorial, polished, warm, and guest-friendly. The site should feel like luxury wedding stationery brought to life online: refined, graceful, personal, and calm.

## Anti-references

Avoid rustic wedding styling, modern-minimalist coldness, too much pink, overly formal or stiff presentation, generic wedding-template patterns, and anything that feels like a corporate website.

## Design Principles

1. Make the site feel like an invitation first and a logistics hub second.
2. Keep guest tasks effortless, especially on mobile.
3. Use romance and polish through restraint, rhythm, typography, and personal detail rather than clutter.
4. Preserve the Mobile, Alabama wedding-weekend sense of place.
5. Let motion feel smooth, slow, and intentional without distracting from important information.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA fundamentals: readable color contrast, comfortable type sizes, keyboard-accessible navigation and controls, clear focus states, large tap targets, no horizontal scrolling, and complete reduced-motion alternatives for guests who prefer less animation.
